Hey, I'm Tyler Wolverton, a software engineer and game programmer. I've had an interest in video games and game development for most of my life. I had the opportunity to work with a student club to make games while pursuing a computer science degree at Purdue University. After graduating in 2014, I worked as a software engineer at Genesys for the next 5 years while continuing to work on game projects in my spare time. I'm currently attending SMU Guildhall pursuing a Software Engineering Master's Degree focusing on game development.

While at Purdue, I was the lead game programmer and later president of ACM SIGGD (Special Interest Group Game Development). I loved the blend of design and engineering that went into working as a game programmer in the group. I contributed to 3 games during my 3 years as a part of the club and was able to progressively take on more responsibility and leadership each year.

My biggest personal project was an action RPG built in my own engine written with SDL2 and OpenGL. Since most of my experience had been related to game programming, working on a game engine alongside the game taught me a lot about the entire game development process.

I'm currently working through the curriculum at SMU Guildhall, a blend of team game projects and solo software development. In my first semester I collaborated with 4 other students to make an Android game, The Gluttonous Grimalkin, using Unity. In my software development courses we built the beginnings of our own 2D game engine by creating 3 small games during the semester, each with more complicated game systems and technology. I've learned an enormous amount so far and I can't wait to continue learning everything I can about game development!

Feel free to check out some of my work and thanks for visiting!
