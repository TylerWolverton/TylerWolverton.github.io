Animal Armies

Spring 2013

Animal Armies was developed using our in house engine, Mirror Engine, and the second version of our level editor, Treequake. In order to bring a roguelike feel to the turn-based strategy genre, each team is comprised of a random assortment of units each with different stats and the world terrain is randomly generated for each match.

I was the technical lead and co-creative director for the project. I coordinated SIGGD's team up with another ACM club SIGART (Special Interest Group Artificial Intelligence) to give the game both local multiplayer and computer controlled opponents. My main technical contribution to the project was the design and development of the core gameplay systems such as how turns are structured and units are moved as well as the GUIs used throughout the game.
