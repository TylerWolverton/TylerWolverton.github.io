20,000 Leagues Over the Sea is a collaboration between SIGGD and GDU (Game Developer's United). Drawing from a wonderful mix between the earlier classics of Missile Defense and Asteroids, 20,000 Leagues Over the Sea revolves around using orbital mechanics to defend a planet from invading aliens. The game was developed with the Unity Game Engine, presented in a top-down 2.5D perspective.

I was the president of SIGGD and lead game programmer for this project. I spent the majority of my time managing both the programmers and artists throughout the development cycle. I also took care of administration tasks such as presentations to corporate partners and other students as well as onboarding new members into SIGGD. I was in charge of importing models and textures created by the artists into the game. I also wrote core gameplay systems like player respawning, planet health and random planet and background generation.

20,000 Leagues Over the Sea was entered into the 2015 Student Independent Games Festival.
